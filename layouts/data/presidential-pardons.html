Presidential Pardons by Offense Severity

An analysis of presidential pardons from George H.W. Bush through Donald Trump, categorized by the severity of the most serious underlying offense. Each pardon is assigned a level from 0 (preemptive) to 5 (violent crime involving death or serious harm).

Source: U.S. Department of Justice Clemency Records

Methodology

Each pardon was categorized based on the most severe charge associated with the individual. Where multiple offenses were present, only the highest-severity charge determined the level. Commutations are excluded; only full presidential pardons are counted.

Preemptive pardons (Level 0) are a distinct category representing blanket pardons issued to individuals for unspecified or anticipated offenses, where no conviction or specific criminal charge was named.

* Donald Trump pardoned approximately 1,500 January 6th defendants en masse on January 20, 2025. These individual pardons are not listed on the DOJ clemency page and are not included in the counts above. If included, Trump's total would exceed 1,700.

Data compiled from the U.S. Department of Justice Office of the Pardon Attorney clemency records. Categorizations reflect the authors' assessment of offense severity and are intended for informational purposes.
