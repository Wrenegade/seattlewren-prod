## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add sliced garlic and cook until golden (not brown), about 2 minutes.

3. Add red pepper flakes and stir for 30 seconds.

4. Add drained pasta directly to the skillet. Toss with the garlic oil, adding pasta water a splash at a time until it coats the noodles.

5. Toss in parsley, season with salt and pepper, and serve with parmesan.

## Notes

This is a classic Roman pantry pasta. The key is slicing the garlic thin and cooking it low and slow — if it burns, start over. The starchy pasta water is what makes the sauce silky.
