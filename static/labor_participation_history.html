Labor Participation vs. Debt-to-GDP Ratio

1948–2023  |  When more people work, GDP grows and the debt ratio shrinks. When they stop, it doesn't.

Sources: BLS via FRED (CIVPART) · OMB via FRED (GFDGDPA188S)

58.8% → 67.1% Labor participation climbed 8.3 points from 1948–2000, driven largely by women entering the workforce. Debt-to-GDP fell from 84% to 55%.
67.1% → 62.5% Since 2000, participation has dropped 4.6 points. Men leaving, boomers retiring, birth rate below replacement. Debt-to-GDP doubled to 119%.
